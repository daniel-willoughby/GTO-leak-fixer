Refining the Zen language

Three evolutions of the current design — all built on the same foundation: cream paper, the sage accent, Fraunces & Inter, and the muted four-colour deck. Same bones, three temperaments. One spot runs through all three so they read side by side: Button vs Big Blind, flop A♠ 8♣ 3♥, you hold A♦ Q♣.

No. 01

Zen · Editorial

A practice almanac, hand-set.

Pushes Zen toward print: a masthead, double rules, an engraved table diagram, a justified drop-cap annotation, a folio and colophon. Decisions are framed as a numbered “ruling.”

Reduces the “AI” feel
  • Asymmetric, editorial layout with real typographic voice
  • Table drawn as a technical figure, not a glossy render
  • Two-colour print restraint inside the four-colour deck
No. 02

Zen · Table

The felt as a tactile centrepiece.

Invests in the app’s signature element. A lit felt with a wooden rail, denominated clay chips, the claret card backs, and a soft drop shadow lift the table into an immersive, premium stage for the decision.

Atmospheric & tactile
  • Refined felt lighting, vignette and grain
  • Real chip stacks with rim spots & a central pot
  • Keeps the cream chrome and two-button decision
No. 03

Zen · Focus

Calm, content-first coaching.

A disciplined system view, shown in the feedback state. A compact hand strip, a clear verdict, an elegant solver-frequency bar, and concept tags make the deepened explanations easy to absorb.

Refined & legible
  • Bespoke solver-strategy bar (78% bet / 22% check)
  • Verdict chip + highlighted “key idea”
  • Tabular figures, generous measure, sticky next
Same palette · same fonts · same deck — only the temperament changes.
